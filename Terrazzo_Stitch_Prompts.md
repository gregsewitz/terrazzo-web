# Terrazzo — Stitch Prompts (Kinetic Dreamer)

Prompts grounded in the Kinetic Dreamer brand identity: Italian Futurist geometry, Depero-inspired boldness, mid-century travel poster warmth, Matisse cutout organic forms. NOT minimalist tech. NOT Swiss grid purity. Dynamic, warm, opinionated, alive.

---

## Global Vibe (Start Here)

> A bold, energetic travel discovery app inspired by Italian Futurist poster art and mid-century travel advertising. Coral (#E86F5A), navy (#1A2D4A), mint teal (#5EC4B2), ochre gold (#E8B84B), and peach (#F4BFA0) used in large, confident flat color fields — not as thin accents. Cream (#FBF5EC) background as canvas. Bold condensed display font for headlines (like League Gothic or Zing Rust), wide grotesque for section headers (like Cy Grotesk Wide), clean DM Sans for body. Layouts use dynamic tension — tilted planes, overlapping geometric forms, asymmetric compositions. Subtle paper grain texture throughout. Feels like a Depero poster meets a modern travel magazine. Nothing sits passively.

## 4. Discover Feed

> Discovery feed for a travel app with Italian Futurist-inspired design. Vertical scroll of place recommendation cards on cream background. Each card uses bold asymmetric layout: photo takes up 60% of the card with a dramatic diagonal crop or angular mask (not a simple rectangle). A large match percentage in bold condensed type (like "94%") overlaps the photo edge in coral. Place name in wide grotesque header font. A one-line editorial take in italic ("The concrete cathedral you didn't know you needed"). Location in small warm gray DM Sans. Cards alternate their photo position left/right for visual rhythm. Decorative geometric accent shapes in mint teal and peach appear between cards. No drop shadows — use bold color fields and overlapping planes for depth.

**Refinement:**
> On the discover feed, add a horizontal filter bar at top with domain chips. Active chip is a solid coral rectangle with cream text. Inactive chips are navy outlined rectangles with navy text. Chips should feel like bold label blocks, not rounded pills.

## Theme Adjustment Prompts

Use these after any screen to push the direction further:

> **More Depero:** "Push the geometric abstraction further. Larger overlapping color field shapes in the background. Angular crops on all images. Headlines should tilt more aggressively. Think Fortunato Depero Campari poster energy."

> **More Poster:** "Make this feel more like a mid-century travel poster. Bolder flat color fields, more dramatic composition, aspirational framing. Think David Klein TWA poster meets a modern app."

> **More Organic:** "Add more Matisse cutout-style biomorphic shapes as accent elements. Softer curves alongside the hard geometry. Peach and mint teal organic forms floating near the edges."

> **More Textural:** "Increase the paper grain and linen texture on all surfaces. Colors should feel slightly chalky, like screen-printed poster ink on rough stock. Nothing should look purely digital."

> **Bolder Color:** "Use the brand colors in larger, more confident fields. Coral and navy should dominate. No thin accent lines — every color should feel like it could hold an entire poster on its own."

> **Image Treatment:** "All photos should have angular geometric masks or diagonal crops — never plain rectangles. At least one edge of every image should be a bold diagonal or angular cut. Overlay partial geometric shapes in brand colors where photos meet the layout."

> **Anti-Tech:** "This is looking too much like a clean tech app. Add more dynamic tension: tilt elements, overlap forms, break the grid. Add paper texture. Replace any rounded pill buttons with bold rectangular blocks. No drop shadows, no glossy effects. Think poster, not SaaS."
